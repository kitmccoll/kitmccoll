# Kit McColl

Computational materials scientist at the University of Oxford and the Faraday Institution. I build ML-accelerated simulation workflows to understand how materials behave at the atomic scale, and use that insight to develop better materials for energy storage and sustainability.

My work spans machine learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs), high-performance computing, and first-principles quantum chemistry. I work closely with experimentalists to connect atomic-scale predictions to real, measurable physical behaviour.

---

## What I work on

- **MLIP development and benchmarking** - building, training, and stress-testing machine learning interatomic potentials (MACE, NequIP, Allegro) for complex materials
- **Configurational disorder** - using cluster expansion and Monte Carlo methods to model disordered and non-equilibrium materials at finite temperature
- **Battery cathode materials** - atomistic modelling of degradation mechanisms in lithium-rich layered oxides, including oxygen loss, phase segregation, and chemical expansion
- **Automated workflows** - scalable simulation pipelines combining DFT, MLIPs, and statistical sampling on GPU HPC infrastructure
